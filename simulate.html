Simulations are Required

Simulation is the most important step, and its used before and after building and launching. It helps users estimate stability, altitude, and whether a design fits the motor and recovery choice.

Why simulate

Reduce guesswork

A simulation helps the user see whether the rocket is likely to remain stable and whether the recovery system is sized appropriately.

What to check

Keep the inputs simple

Check rocket length, weight, fin shape, motor class, and the expected launch conditions before moving ahead.

Simulation topics

Good for users who want to plan first.

Stability

See if the center of gravity and fin layout are likely to behave well in flight.

Altitude

Estimate how high the rocket may go so the launch field and recovery system make sense.

Motor fit

Compare motor choices before buying hardware or committing to a launch.

Recovery

Check whether the recovery system is appropriate for the estimated flight profile.
