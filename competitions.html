Beginner path

Start with simple contest formats

Competition does not have to mean advanced hardware. Many entry formats reward accuracy, planning, and safe execution.

What matters

Build skill, not complexity

The best first competition path is the one that helps a user practice launch preparation, recovery, and repeatable results.

Competition types

Use the challenge that matches the user.

Altitude goal

Useful when the user wants to compare design choices and motor performance.

Accuracy tasks

Reward careful launch planning, recovery prep, and repeatable flying.

Team events

Good for clubs, schools, and new rocketeers who want a shared project.

Design challenges

For users who want a stricter goal and a clearer performance target.
